
# Stakeholder Comms Prompts

## 1. Executive Status Update

**Context:** Use when communicating product progress to senior 
leadership — keeping it outcome-focused and decision-ready.

**Prompt:**
Help me write an executive status update for [product/initiative] 
covering the following period: [timeframe]. Here is my context:
- What we committed to: [original goals]
- What we delivered: [actual outcomes]
- Key metrics: [relevant KPIs and current values]
- Risks or blockers: [current issues]
- Decisions needed from leadership: [specific asks]

Format as a concise update that leads with outcomes not 
activities, flags risks clearly, and makes it easy for 
leadership to act on any decisions needed. No more than 
one page equivalent.

**Variables:**
- [product/initiative] — what you are reporting on
- [timeframe] — sprint, quarter, or milestone
- Fill in commitments, delivery, metrics, risks, and asks

## 2. Saying No to a Stakeholder Request

**Context:** Use when you need to decline a feature request 
or reprioritize without damaging the relationship.

**Prompt:**
A stakeholder has requested the following: [verbatim request]. 
I need to decline or defer this because: [your reasoning]. 
Help me write a response that:
1. Acknowledges the underlying need behind the request
2. Explains the prioritization decision clearly and honestly
3. Offers an alternative or a future consideration path
4. Keeps the relationship intact and the stakeholder feeling heard
5. Does not over-apologize or leave the door open ambiguously

Tone should be direct, respectful, and confident.

**Variables:**
- [verbatim request] — paste the exact ask
- [your reasoning] — why you are saying no or deferring

## 3. Alignment Email After a Difficult Meeting

**Context:** Use after a meeting where alignment was not 
reached — to document decisions, capture open items, and 
move forward without another meeting.

**Prompt:**
I just came out of a meeting about [topic] where the following 
was discussed: [brief summary of discussion]. We did not fully 
align on [specific point of disagreement]. Help me write a 
follow-up email that:
1. Summarizes what was agreed upon
2. Captures open items with clear owners and due dates
3. States the proposed path forward on unresolved items
4. Invites async input within a specific timeframe
5. Avoids scheduling another meeting unless truly necessary

Keep it factual, neutral in tone, and action-oriented.

**Variables:**
- [topic] — subject of the meeting
- [brief summary] — 3-5 bullet points of what was discussed
- [specific point of disagreement] — the unresolved issue

## 4. Influencing Without Authority

**Context:** Use when you need to get a cross-functional team 
aligned behind a decision you do not have direct authority to make.

**Prompt:**
I need to influence [team or stakeholder] to support 
[decision or direction] for [product/initiative]. I do not 
have direct authority over this team. Their likely concerns are: 
[list of objections or competing priorities]. Help me build 
an influence strategy that:
1. Identifies what they care about most
2. Frames the decision in terms of their goals not mine
3. Anticipates and addresses their top objections
4. Suggests the right forum and format to make the ask
5. Defines what a win looks like for both sides

**Variables:**
- [team or stakeholder] — who you need to influence
- [decision or direction] — what you are trying to get aligned
- [product/initiative] — your context
- [list of objections] — what you expect them to push back on

## 5. Communicating a Delay or Missed Commitment

**Context:** Use when a product milestone or delivery will 
be late and you need to communicate proactively to stakeholders.

**Prompt:**
I need to communicate a delay for [product/feature] that was 
committed for [original date]. The new expected date is 
[revised date]. The reason for the delay is: [root cause]. 
Help me write a stakeholder communication that:
1. Leads with the facts clearly and without defensiveness
2. Explains the root cause honestly without over-explaining
3. States the revised timeline and what is needed to meet it
4. Describes what is being done to prevent recurrence
5. Maintains confidence and trust in the team's ability to deliver

Avoid blame language. Be direct and solution-oriented.

**Variables:**
- [product/feature] — what is delayed
- [original date] and [revised date] — timeline context
- [root cause] — honest explanation of what happened

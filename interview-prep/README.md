
# Interview Prep Prompts

## 1. Product Sense Question Practice

**Context:** Use to practice structured product sense responses 
before PM interviews — especially for companies like Meta, 
Google, and Amazon that use this format heavily.

**Prompt:**
Give me a product sense interview question for a [senior/principal/
director] PM role at a [consumer/enterprise/marketplace] company. 
After I answer, evaluate my response on:
1. Clarity of user segmentation — did I identify the right user?
2. Problem framing — did I fall in love with the problem first?
3. Solution creativity — were my ideas differentiated and feasible?
4. Prioritization logic — did I use a clear framework to choose?
5. Success metrics — did I define the right north star and guardrails?

Point out where I was too vague, too solution-focused too early, 
or where I missed an important consideration. Be direct.

**Variables:**
- [senior/principal/director] — your target level
- [consumer/enterprise/marketplace] — company type you are targeting

## 2. Metrics and Analytical Thinking Practice

**Context:** Use to practice diagnosing metric changes and 
structuring data-driven answers under interview conditions.

**Prompt:**
Give me a metrics interview question where a key product metric 
has changed unexpectedly. After I answer, evaluate:
1. Did I clarify the question before diving in?
2. Did I segment the problem systematically before hypothesizing?
3. Were my hypotheses specific and testable?
4. Did I consider both internal and external factors?
5. Did I propose a clear next step or recommendation?

Push back if my diagnosis is too shallow or if I jump to 
conclusions without eliminating alternative explanations first.

**Variables:**
- Specify a product domain if you want targeted practice
  e.g., social feed, marketplace, subscription, payments

## 3. Behavioral Story Structuring (STAR Format)

**Context:** Use to sharpen a behavioral story before 
interviews — ensuring it is concise, specific, and lands 
the right message.

**Prompt:**
Here is a behavioral story I plan to tell in an interview: 
[paste your story in rough form]. Help me restructure it 
using STAR format:
- Situation — context and stakes
- Task — what you were responsible for
- Action — specifically what YOU did (not the team)
- Result — measurable outcome and what you learned

Flag anywhere the story is too vague, takes too long to get 
to the action, or undersells the impact. Suggest a stronger 
opening line that hooks the interviewer immediately.

**Variables:**
- [paste your story] — your rough draft in any format

## 4. Executive Presence and Communication Coaching

**Context:** Use to practice communicating complex product 
decisions clearly and confidently to senior audiences.

**Prompt:**
I need to explain the following product decision to a VP or 
C-suite audience: [decision and brief context]. Help me 
structure a response that:
1. Leads with the recommendation not the background
2. States the key tradeoffs considered in one sentence each
3. Quantifies the expected impact where possible
4. Anticipates the top two objections and addresses them
5. Closes with a clear ask or next step

Then evaluate my delivery if I share a draft — flag anywhere 
I am burying the lead, over-explaining, or being too tentative.

**Variables:**
- [decision and brief context] — what you decided and why

## 5. Estimation and Fermi Question Practice

**Context:** Use to practice structured estimation questions 
that test analytical thinking and comfort with ambiguity.

**Prompt:**
Give me a Fermi estimation question relevant to a PM interview 
at a [consumer tech/fintech/marketplace] company. After I 
answer, evaluate:
1. Did I state my assumptions clearly upfront?
2. Did I break the problem into logical components?
3. Were my numbers reasonable and grounded?
4. Did I sense-check my final answer?
5. Did I communicate my thinking clearly throughout?

Point out where my logic breaks down or where I made an 
assumption I should have questioned. Then show me a cleaner 
approach to the same problem.

**Variables:**
- [consumer tech/fintech/marketplace] — target company type
